## Angle 1 — RC vs. Backstage ("we don't charge you to be seen")
- On **Backstage**, the *dancer* pays a subscription (~$150–$180/yr, confirm) **just to be allowed to apply** to jobs. It's a general-market casting board.
- On **Relevé Connect**, the **$149 Professional** membership **showcases them as talent** (a real, curated profile), **includes The Beat** (browse + apply, no per-application nickel-and-diming), gives access to the Roster, The Climb, and member events — and offers **Swing so they can actually MAKE money.**
- The line: *Backstage charges you to look for work. Relevé gets you seen, gets you hired, and helps you earn — for about the same money you already spend.*

## Angle 2 — RC vs. the Union ("the wage floor you can't reach")
- The union offers a **wage floor** (confirm current minimum before citing a number) — **but** you **pay dues**, and **most working dancers can't even get in** (gatekept eligibility).
- Relevé Connect is **open to every working dancer**, takes **no cut of the artist's wage** (no-tax-on-labor guardrail), and still raises professional standards through vetting, reviews, and a real credentialed presence.
- The line: *A wage floor does nothing for the dancers who can't get through the door. Relevé opens the door — and never taxes your paycheck.*
