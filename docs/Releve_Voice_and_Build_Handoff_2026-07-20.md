## 7. Operational fix-list — before inviting people

**🔴 #1 PRIORITY — the acceptance → profile → pay funnel is broken (confirmed live, July 20).** When the founder approves an applicant, the applicant currently receives **nothing** — no acceptance email, no "build your profile" directions, no payment instructions. Backend confirms the approval *did* process (added to the "tier-approved" MailerLite group at 10:32:33) but sent **no email** and gave **no on-screen confirmation** (the green "Approve" button just stays lit). This breaks the funnel at the exact revenue moment: accepted people don't know they're in, can't build a profile, and can't pay.
  → **Claude Code:** the approval action must send a **reliable transactional acceptance email** — same mechanism as the working "application received" email (lib/mailer.ts), **NOT** a MailerLite welcome automation (those silently suppress for any email already on the list, which is why the test got nothing). The email must contain: (1) you're accepted, (2) a link + directions to **build your profile**, (3) instructions/link to **pay**. Confirm the approval handler actually calls this email — right now it updates the MailerLite group but skips the email. Also make the **Approve button change state** on success (→ "Approved ✓").
  → **Open question for Kathleen:** is a **payment step built at all yet?** (Stripe was never set up.) If not, "instructions to pay" can't exist until the payment mechanism is decided and built — that is the monetization piece to design next, and it gates launch.

**The application:**
- **PRIMARY-ROLE FIX (confirmed live, July 20).** Selecting a secondary role forces a fully *required* specifics section for it. Reproduced: primary role = Teacher, but at **Step 6 of 11 the app still demands "Studio Owner Specifics"** — Studio name, City/State, Year founded, all required (*) — just because "Studio Owner" was also checked. This is a real drop-off point, and it punishes exactly the multi-hat professionals (a teacher who also runs a studio) you most want.
  → **Claude Code:** make only the **primary** role's specifics section required. Any additional roles the applicant selected → make those sections optional, or defer them to the profile after acceptance. A secondary role's section must never block submission. (The role-branching architecture itself is good — this is a required-vs-optional setting, not a rebuild.)
- **STUDIO = EMPLOYER, not talent (confirmed live, July 20).** In "Studio Owner Specifics," *student count* and *number of teachers on staff* are correct employer questions ✅ — but it also **requires "Which styles feel comfortable to teach?"**, a talent question that doesn't belong on the employer side. → **Claude Code:** remove the teaching-styles question from the Studio Owner path. A studio is an employer; we don't collect what classes it offers. Audit the whole studio path to ask employer questions only (who they hire for, size, location) — never "what do *you* teach."
- **DUPLICATE-EMAIL is rejected at the END — move it to the START (confirmed live, July 20).** The block ("an application has already been submitted with this email") fires at **Section 13 of 13, after everything is filled.** The message is fine; the timing is brutal. → **Claude Code:** validate email uniqueness at **Step 1 (Identity & Contact)**, the moment it's entered — never after the applicant completes the form. Keep the graceful message + contact line. (Policy: unique email per *person* is correct and stays; one person with multiple roles is fine; two different people need separate emails; studios may later warrant their own org/employer account type.)
- **Possible validation glitch:** Studio name field showed a typed value ("kathleens") but still flagged "Studio name is required." Worth a check.
- **Stage it** (essentials first, deeper vetting for placement) — the primary/secondary rule above is the biggest shortening lever, with zero loss of rigor.
